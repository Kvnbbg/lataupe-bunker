Life Underground: Our New Home

Humanity is forced to retreat beneath the surface, like moles seeking refuge.

Underground cities and tunnel networks become our new habitat.

Artificial lighting mimics day/night cycles to maintain circadian rhythms.

Water recycling and vertical farming provide sustainable resources.

Psychological challenges of confined living lead to new social structures.

Surface excursions require specialized protective gear, limited to essential tasks only.

Like moles that evolved for underground living, humans must adapt to subterranean existence - developing enhanced low-light vision, efficient resource usage, and complex tunnel navigation systems.
