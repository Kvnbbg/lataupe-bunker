The Vanishing Shield

The ozone layer has been Earth's natural protective shield for millions of years.

It absorbs 97-99% of the sun's medium-frequency ultraviolet light, which would otherwise damage exposed life forms.

Scientific projections show the Antarctic ozone hole was expected to close by 2060s.

But increased space travel, volcanic eruptions, and continued emissions have accelerated depletion.

Without intervention, we face a future where our shield completely vanishes.

Without the Montreal Protocol, by the end of 21st century UV levels around the globe would have exceeded levels previously experienced even in the most extreme environments.
